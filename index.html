This study challenges conventional wisdom about when Medicaid's mental-health benefits begin. Using causal forest methodology on the Oregon Health Insurance Experiment, the research reveals that positive effects emerge within six months—but only for the most economically vulnerable recipients.
Methodology: Causal forest analysis of 5,635 participants from baseline and six-month OHIE surveys. Mental health measured using standardized proxy combining depression diagnosis, mental health days, and general health status. Income emerged as third most important predictor of treatment heterogeneity.
